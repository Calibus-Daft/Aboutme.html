About Nick "Goose" Bradshaw

Nick “Goose” Bradshaw was a Radar Intercept Officer in the United States Navy, best known for flying alongside Lt. Pete “Maverick” Mitchell in the F-14 Tomcat. He was respected not only for his skills in the air but also for his loyalty, humor, and warm personality.

Professional Background

Goose spent his career in the cockpit, helping pilots navigate high-speed, high-stakes aerial maneuvers. He had a knack for reading the radar and staying calm when things got intense — qualities that made him an exceptional Radar Intercept Officer.

He trained at TOPGUN, where he honed his skills and built a reputation for reliability and quick thinking. Goose wasn’t just following procedures, he brought creativity and energy to every mission, making him a trusted partner in the sky.

Skills and Achievements

Goose was highly skilled at radar systems, navigation, and in-flight communication. But beyond technical abilities, he had a talent for teamwork, making every pilot feel confident and supported. His quick wit and calm presence often turned stressful moments into manageable ones.

He participated in countless training exercises and missions, leaving a legacy of competence and camaraderie. Those who worked with him remember him as someone who could always be counted on, both professionally and personally.

Personal Life

Outside the cockpit, Goose was a devoted husband and father. He loved spending time with his wife, Carole, and their son, Bradley, cherishing moments that grounded (pun intended) him despite the high-pressure nature of his work.

He also had a playful side, often enjoying music and lighthearted fun with friends. Whether at home or on duty, Goose brought warmth, humor, and loyalty to every relationship he valued.
